Service group

Full bathroom remodeling

A complete remodel coordinates the room as one system: layout, water, power, ventilation, surfaces, storage, lighting, and finish quality.

Best for

  • Outdated primary or guest baths.
  • Layouts with poor storage or awkward fixture placement.
  • Rooms with past leaks, damaged floors, or ventilation issues.
  • Resale-focused upgrades needing coordinated finish quality.
Detailed scope

Services included in a full remodel path.

Planning and project definition

Partner discussions typically begin with measurements, room constraints, desired fixture locations, storage goals, moisture concerns, accessibility needs, and finish direction. The goal is to identify whether the project is cosmetic, semi-custom, or trade-intensive.

Demolition and site preparation

Local remodelers may remove tubs, showers, vanities, tile, drywall, flooring, mirrors, and fixtures while protecting adjacent rooms. Discovery can reveal framing, plumbing, subfloor, ventilation, or water damage conditions that affect scope.

Plumbing, electrical, and ventilation coordination

Full remodels often include valve replacement, drain changes, toilet relocation checks, GFCI outlets, vanity lighting, exhaust fan upgrades, heated floor wiring, and code-aware spacing. Licensed trades may be required by local rules.

Surface systems and finish installation

Methods can include cement board or foam backer systems, bonded waterproofing membranes, shower pan preparation, uncoupling membranes, large-format tile leveling, grout selection, caulk movement joints, and clean transitions at doors.

Final assembly and quality review

Fixtures, mirrors, glass, hardware, accessories, paint, trim, and punch-list work complete the space. A strong partner should review drainage, door swings, sealant lines, outlet placement, lighting warmth, and user comfort.

Common materialsPorcelain tile, quartz tops, acrylic or cast tubs, glass doors, brass or nickel fixtures, waterproof wall systems.
Typical methodsLayout mapping, moisture mitigation, substrate prep, waterproofing, tile setting, fixture commissioning.
