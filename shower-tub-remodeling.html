Wet-area services

Shower and tub remodeling

Wet-area projects need careful product selection, slope, waterproofing, valve access, glass planning, and day-to-day comfort.

Services

  • Tub-to-shower conversions.
  • Custom tile showers and shower surrounds.
  • Frameless glass doors and panels.
  • Soaking tub and alcove tub replacement.
Methods and materials

Built around water control first, finishes second.

Conversion planning

Partners evaluate existing tub footprints, drain location, valve height, entry width, curb requirements, bench placement, and whether the project should use a prefabricated system, tile shower, or hybrid approach.

Waterproofing systems

Common methods include foam or cementitious backer board, bonded sheet membranes, liquid-applied membranes, pre-sloped pans, flood testing where appropriate, and careful sealing around niches, benches, and penetrations.

Fixtures and controls

Projects may include pressure-balanced or thermostatic valves, diverters, handheld shower bars, rain heads, body sprays, tub fillers, linear drains, and accessible control placement.

Glass and finishing

Frameless glass requires clean tile planes, curb and threshold planning, door swing clearance, hardware finish coordination, and maintenance expectations around squeegeeing and water spotting.

Best outcomesCleaner bathing access, improved leak resistance, stronger visual focal point, easier maintenance.
Watch pointsDrain slope, waterproofing continuity, glass clearance, valve compatibility, niche height, tub weight.
