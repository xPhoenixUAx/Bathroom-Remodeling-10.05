Surface systems

Bathroom tile and flooring

Tile work should look calm and precise because the preparation below it is disciplined.

Tile scopes

  • Floor and wall tile installation.
  • Heated floor systems.
  • Mosaic, niche, border, and accent layouts.
  • Threshold and room transition planning.
Craft detail

Methods that make premium tile feel intentional.

Substrate and leveling

Installers evaluate subfloor stiffness, wall flatness, old adhesive, moisture exposure, crack isolation, uncoupling membranes, and the need for self-leveling underlayment before tile layout begins.

Material selection

Porcelain is durable and moisture-friendly, ceramic works well for many walls, natural stone needs sealing discipline, mosaics improve shower floor traction, and large-format tile needs flatter surfaces and skilled handling.

Setting and grout systems

Methods may include appropriate thinset selection, back-buttering large tile, leveling clips, expansion gaps, movement joints, epoxy or high-performance grout, and color-matched sealants at plane changes.

Slip resistance and maintenance

Bathroom flooring should balance texture, cleanability, bare-foot comfort, and water exposure. Partners can discuss matte finishes, grout width, heated floor compatibility, and cleaning expectations.

Common materialsPorcelain, ceramic, mosaic sheets, stone-look slab tile, epoxy grout, uncoupling membranes.
MethodsLayout dry-fit, substrate prep, waterproofing tie-ins, leveling, grout sealing, final lippage review.
