Daily-use design

Vanities, storage and lighting

The vanity wall carries the routines: grooming, storage, outlets, mirror clarity, and the calm feeling of an organized room.

Services

  • Single and double vanity replacement.
  • Linen towers, drawer storage, and recessed medicine cabinets.
  • Sconces, backlit mirrors, ceiling lighting, and controls.
  • Outlet planning, GFCI placement, and appliance storage.
Functional polish

Storage and lighting should solve small daily frictions.

Vanity selection

Projects may use stock, semi-custom, or custom cabinetry. Partners assess plumbing centerlines, drawer clearance, wall blocking, countertop overhangs, sink style, faucet holes, and moisture-resistant finishes.

Countertops and sinks

Quartz, solid surface, stone, porcelain slabs, undermount sinks, vessel sinks, integrated tops, and backsplash details should be chosen around cleaning, splash control, and the visual weight of the room.

Lighting design

Good bathroom lighting uses layers: flattering face lighting at mirrors, quiet ambient light, shower-safe fixtures where needed, warm color temperature, dimming, and careful control placement.

Storage strategy

Drawers, towers, recessed cabinets, towel storage, hair tool outlets, laundry pullouts, and open shelves can make a small bathroom feel larger without adding visual clutter.

Common materialsWalnut or painted cabinetry, quartz, brass, nickel, framed mirrors, LED systems, moisture-rated finishes.
MethodsCenterline checks, blocking, electrical planning, scribe fitting, countertop templating, fixture alignment.
