2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are commonly used to make websites work efficiently, remember preferences, support form functionality, and provide website owners with information about how visitors use a site.

Cookies may be session cookies, persistent cookies, first-party cookies set by our website, or third-party cookies set by outside services used on the website.

Analytics cookies. These cookies help us understand website traffic, page views, user behavior, technical issues, and performance opportunities. Analytics data helps us improve the website experience and project inquiry flow.

Functional cookies. These cookies support enhanced functionality, such as remembering preferences or temporarily supporting form interactions. Some functional cookies may be set by third-party providers whose tools are embedded on our pages.

You can accept, reject, delete, or block cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers accept cookies automatically, but you can usually modify your settings to decline cookies or notify you when cookies are being placed.

You may also use opt-out tools provided by analytics or advertising organizations, such as Google Analytics opt-out tools, the Digital Advertising Alliance, or the Network Advertising Initiative.
